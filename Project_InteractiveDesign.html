Life Flower

Project Overview

Raise awareness about how we squander time and encourage reflection on life and its fleeting nature.

Artist Statement:


This project visualizes the passage of time and life through an interactive kinetic sculpture that combines heart rate data, mechanical flowers, LED lights, and a clock. The blooming and wilting flowers symbolize life's cycles, while the ticking clock reflects the unstoppable flow of time. Light represents growth, hope, and the influence of culture and time on our experiences. The piece invites reflection on the fleeting nature of time, urging viewers to consider its preciousness and how it shapes their lives.

Detail

Core Concept:


The system aims to represent time, life, and their cyclical nature. It will use heart rate data to drive the motion of clock hands, control the blooming of mechanical flowers, and activate LED lights. These elements will symbolize the irreversibility of time, the transient nature of life, and the inheritance of culture.

Messages:


Clock & Heart Rate: Time flows continuously, and it cannot be stopped, just like the heartbeat. Mechanical Flowers: Flowers symbolize birth and death, with their blooming and wilting representing the beginning and end of life. LED Lights: The lights symbolize life and hope, adding a universal layer of meaning associated with light's symbolic role in many cultures. Processing Scene: The butterflies in the scene, changing colors based on heart rate, represent the emotional aspect of time and life, implying that mood and vitality are tied to time.

System Design


The task flow shows how the user interacts with the system, starting from wearing the heart rate sensor to observing physical reactions (like the movement of clock hands, LEDs, and mechanical flowers) driven by the data collected.

The wireframe provides a visual of the software interface, indicating the connection between hardware and software.

The user flow diagram shows how the user will engage with both the hardware and software components.

Prototype1

3D Model

The flower model includes petal, axis, and connecting components. A mechanical design using servos and strings controls the opening and closing of the petals, symbolizing life stages.

Hardware Design

The prototype tests different ways to control servos using the heart rate sensor, with various approaches to mapping heart rate values to servo movements.

Software Design

The system uses Arduino to read heart rate data and control the movement of servos. Data is mapped to smaller intervals to activate corresponding servos and create synchronized movement.

Mechanical Flower Design

Iron strings are used to make the flower petals, and polish is applied to fill gaps, ensuring the petals are stable during movement

User Feedback

Based on user feedback, improvements were suggested for simplifying the 3D printed models and improving the balance and stability of the mechanical flower. Additionally, issues with sensor stability and the handling of servo data were noted.

Prototype2

3D Model

The flower model is simplified, focusing on a vertical floral axis rather than complex petal models. This helps reduce material usage and printing time.

Hardware Design

The number of servos is reduced to four (representing clock positions: 12:00, 3:00, 6:00, 9:00). Each servo is activated based on heart rate data ranges, and LED lights are used to represent different heart rate intervals.

Software Design

The system reads the heart rate sensor data, maps it to intervals, and activates corresponding servos and LED lights. A 360-degree servo is used to control the clock hand's movement.

Mechanical Flower Design

The redesigned mechanical flower features improved petal rotation with balanced loops and a clock face setup incorporating servo motors and LED lights for enhanced functionality and aesthetics.
